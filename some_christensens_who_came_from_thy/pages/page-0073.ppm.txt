61 Emigration

the departing Danes took a long, last look at the
"Lovely Land" and sailed away, they were leaving
behind the country that had cradled their kinfolk
for a thousand years and the forces which had
woven each everlasting fibre into the tapestry of
their very beings. They crossed more than an
ocean and a continent when they said goodby to
Jutland and her world of green islands; they
crossed latitudes of blood and longitudes of the
mind, Things would never be the same for them
again as they turned their faces west and set
sail toward their ''Promised Land",
